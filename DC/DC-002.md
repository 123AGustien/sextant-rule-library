# DC-002 — Power Instability

## Rule Information

Rule ID: DC-002

Domain: Data Centre Resilience

Category: Power Infrastructure

Status: Active

Version: 1.0

Repository: Sextant Rule Library

---

## Purpose

Evaluate the resilience of a data centre during power instability events and assess potential cascading effects across interconnected operational, cyber, infrastructure, and financial domains.

---

## Scenario

The primary electrical supply experiences instability due to voltage fluctuations, frequency deviations, grid disturbances, or partial power loss. Backup power systems may be required to maintain operational continuity.

The scenario evaluates:

- Utility power stability
- UPS performance
- Generator availability
- Power distribution
- Critical equipment operation
- Service continuity

---

## Primary Indicators

- Utility power status
- Voltage stability
- Frequency stability
- UPS load
- UPS battery capacity
- Generator operational status
- Power Distribution Unit (PDU) status
- Critical power alarms

---

## Risk Levels

### GREEN

Power infrastructure operating normally.

Primary and backup power systems are fully available.

No contingency actions required.

---

### YELLOW

Early signs of power instability.

Monitor:

- Voltage fluctuations
- UPS load
- Battery health
- Generator readiness

Action:

Increase monitoring and verify backup power readiness.

---

### ORANGE

Significant power instability.

Indicators:

- Repeated voltage fluctuations
- UPS operating under elevated load
- Generator on standby
- Increased operational risk

Potential Cascade:

Power Instability

↓

UPS Dependency

↓

Reduced Power Margin

↓

Operational Stress

Action:

Prepare for generator activation, reduce non-critical loads, and monitor critical infrastructure continuously.

---

### RED

Critical power failure.

Indicators:

- Utility power loss
- UPS capacity approaching limits
- Generator failure or overload
- Critical infrastructure alarms

Potential Cascade:

Power Failure

↓

Critical Equipment Shutdown

↓

Service Outage

↓

Data Centre Disruption

↓

Cross-Domain Systemic Risk

Action:

Activate emergency power procedures, initiate disaster recovery where necessary, and protect critical services.

---

## Cross-Domain Dependencies

Data Centre (DC)

↓

Infrastructure (INF)

↓

Cyber (CYB)

↓

Financial (FIN)

Power instability may disrupt digital infrastructure, communication systems, cybersecurity operations, and financial services.

---

## Contingency Actions

- Monitor electrical systems continuously
- Verify UPS capacity and battery health
- Test generator readiness
- Reduce non-essential electrical loads
- Prioritise critical services
- Escalate to incident management when thresholds are exceeded
