# DC-009 — Cooling Load Saturation

## Rule Information

Rule ID: DC-009

Domain: Data Centre Resilience

Category: Thermal Capacity Stress

Status: Active

Version: 1.0

Repository: Sextant Rule Library

---

## Purpose

Evaluate data centre resilience when cooling capacity becomes saturated due to sustained high thermal loads, and assess cascading impacts across compute, power efficiency, and system stability.

---

## Scenario

Cooling infrastructure operates near or at maximum capacity due to prolonged compute demand, high ambient temperatures, or inefficient airflow distribution. This leads to reduced cooling effectiveness and increased risk of thermal instability.

The scenario evaluates:

- Cooling capacity utilisation
- Thermal headroom
- Heat rejection efficiency
- Airflow balance
- Rack density stress
- Environmental load conditions

---

## Primary Indicators

- Cooling system utilisation (%)
- Inlet/outlet temperature differential
- Hot aisle / cold aisle imbalance
- Chiller load percentage
- Fan speed saturation
- Environmental ambient temperature
- PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness)

---

## Risk Levels

### GREEN

Cooling systems operating within optimal capacity.

Adequate thermal headroom is maintained.

No contingency actions required.

---

### YELLOW

Approaching cooling capacity limits.

Monitor:

- Rising ambient temperature
- Increased chiller load
- Reduced thermal headroom

Action:

Prepare for load redistribution and optimise airflow management.

---

### ORANGE

Cooling capacity under sustained pressure.

Indicators:

- Near-maximal cooling utilization
- Thermal imbalance zones
- Increasing rack-level hotspots
- Reduced cooling efficiency

Potential Cascade:

Cooling Saturation

↓

Thermal Buildup

↓

Compute Throttling

↓

Service Performance Degradation

Action:

Reduce compute load, optimise airflow, and prioritise critical systems cooling.

---

### RED

Critical cooling saturation.

Indicators:

- Cooling system fully saturated
- Persistent hotspots
- Rapid temperature escalation
- Risk of hardware damage

Potential Cascade:

Thermal Saturation

↓

System Overheating

↓

Emergency Shutdown

↓

Infrastructure Disruption

↓

Cross-Domain Systemic Risk

Action:

Initiate emergency load shedding, activate additional cooling resources, and protect critical infrastructure immediately.

---

Cooling saturation may propagate operational instability and systemic risk across connected domains.

---

## Contingency Actions

- Monitor thermal headroom continuously
- Reduce non-critical compute loads
- Increase cooling efficiency measures
- Redistribute workload across racks
- Activate auxiliary cooling systems if available
- Escalate if saturation persists or worsens
