# DC-006 — Cooling Recovery Failure

## Rule Information

Rule ID: DC-006

Domain: Data Centre Resilience

Category: Thermal Recovery / Post-Incident Stability

Status: Active

Version: 1.0

Repository: Sextant Rule Library

---

## Purpose

Evaluate data centre resilience during post-cooling failure recovery, focusing on unstable thermal normalization, delayed cooling system restoration, and cascading infrastructure instability.

---

## Scenario

A cooling system failure has occurred and primary cooling capacity has been partially or fully restored. However, thermal stability is not immediately achieved due to residual heat, uneven airflow distribution, or delayed system recalibration.

The scenario evaluates:

- Thermal normalization rate
- Cooling system reactivation stability
- Hotspot persistence
- Server recovery sequencing
- Load redistribution under thermal stress

---

## Primary Indicators

- Server inlet temperature
- Rack-level thermal variance
- Cooling unit restart stability
- Airflow consistency
- Hotspot detection frequency
- System throttling frequency
- Workload redistribution activity

---

## Risk Levels

### GREEN

Thermal conditions fully stabilised.

Cooling recovery is complete and system operates within safe temperature thresholds.

No contingency actions required.

---

### YELLOW

Early-stage thermal recovery instability.

Monitor:

- Temperature fluctuations
- Cooling unit performance
- Localised hotspots

Action:

Continue monitoring and verify stable airflow restoration.

---

### ORANGE

Persistent thermal instability during recovery.

Indicators:

- Uneven cooling distribution
- Intermittent hotspots
- CPU throttling events
- Cooling system inconsistency

Potential Cascade:

Cooling Recovery Instability

↓

Performance Throttling

↓

Workload Imbalance

↓

Service Degradation

Action:

Rebalance workloads, optimise cooling distribution, and prioritise thermal stabilisation.

---

### RED

Critical failure in cooling recovery phase.

Indicators:

- Re-emerging overheating
- Cooling system instability
- Rapid thermal escalation
- Risk of hardware damage

Potential Cascade:

Cooling Instability

↓

Thermal Runaway Risk

↓

System Shutdown

↓

Infrastructure Stress

↓

Cross-Domain Systemic Risk

Action:

Initiate emergency thermal protection, reduce system load immediately, and escalate to disaster recovery procedures.

Thermal instability during recovery may impact operational continuity and propagate across interconnected systems.

---

## Contingency Actions

- Monitor post-recovery thermal stability continuously
- Rebalance compute workloads
- Inspect cooling system recalibration
- Activate additional cooling support if required
- Prevent thermal hotspots from recurring
- Escalate if instability persists beyond thresholds
