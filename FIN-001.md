# FIN-001 — FX Stress (SGD/IDR)

## Scenario
Foreign capital outflow causing currency pressure between SGD and IDR.

---

## Trigger Conditions
- Sustained foreign bond selling in Indonesia
- Increased global risk-off sentiment
- Capital outflows exceeding normal range

---

## Indicators
- SGD/IDR exchange rate movement
- USD Index (DXY)
- Indonesian government bond yields
- Foreign exchange reserves trend
- Market liquidity conditions

---

## Threshold Logic

### GREEN
- Stable exchange rate movement
- Normal market liquidity
- No abnormal capital flow

### YELLOW
- Mild SGD strength vs IDR
- Early capital outflow signals
- Slight increase in volatility

### ORANGE
- Sustained IDR depreciation
- Active foreign bond outflows
- Rising bond yields

### RED
- Severe currency depreciation
- Liquidity tightening
- Combined FX + bond market stress

---

## Expected Cascade

Capital outflow  
→ Currency depreciation  
→ Import cost increase  
→ Inflation pressure  
→ Interest rate increase  
→ Liquidity tightening  
→ Economic slowdown risk

---

## Contingency Actions
- Increase FX monitoring frequency
- Review liquidity buffers
- Notify treasury operations team
- Run stress simulation scenarios
- Assess hedging positions

---

## Recovery Actions
- Stabilise liquidity conditions
- Rebalance FX exposure
- Access contingency funding lines if required
- Restore normal monitoring state

---

## Audit Notes
This rule is for simulation and resilience assessment only. It does not predict actual market outcomes.
