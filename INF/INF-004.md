# INF-004 — Power Grid Instability (External Dependency)

## Rule Information

Rule ID: INF-004

Domain: Infrastructure Resilience

Category: External Power Dependency Failure

Status: Active

Version: 1.0

Repository: Sextant Rule Library

---

## Purpose

Evaluate system resilience under external power grid instability affecting data centre operations, network infrastructure, and dependent digital services.

---

## Scenario

Regional or national power grid instability causes fluctuating power supply, frequency instability, or partial outages affecting infrastructure dependent on external electricity providers.

The scenario evaluates:

- Power supply stability
- UPS and generator activation
- Load transfer response
- Infrastructure continuity under instability
- Cascade into thermal and compute stress

---

## Primary Indicators

- Voltage fluctuation events
- Grid frequency instability
- UPS activation frequency
- Generator load engagement
- Power redundancy utilization
- Partial facility shutdown events
- Equipment restart frequency

---

## Risk Levels

### GREEN

Stable power grid supply.

Infrastructure operating normally.

No contingency actions required.

---

### YELLOW

Minor grid instability detected.

Monitor:

- Voltage fluctuations
- Short power interruptions
- UPS engagement frequency

Action:

Verify backup power readiness.

---

### ORANGE

Significant power instability.

Indicators:

- Frequent grid fluctuations
- Increased UPS reliance
- Partial power interruptions
- Load instability across systems

Potential Cascade:

Power Instability

↓

Cooling Stress

↓

Compute Degradation

↓

Service Disruption

Action:

Activate backup generators and stabilize power distribution.

---

### RED

Critical power grid failure.

Indicators:

- Sustained power outages
- Loss of external grid supply
- Full UPS and generator dependency
- Infrastructure instability

Potential Cascade:

Power Failure

↓

Data Centre Disruption

↓

Service Outage

↓

Cross-Domain Systemic Risk

Action:

Switch to full backup power systems, reduce load, and initiate emergency infrastructure protocols.

---

## Cross-Domain Dependencies

Infrastructure (INF)

↓

Data Centre (DC)

↓

Cyber (CYB)

↓

Financial (FIN)

Power instability affects all downstream systems including cyber security monitoring and financial transaction systems.

---

## Contingency Actions

- Activate backup generators immediately
- Balance load across power systems
- Monitor UPS and battery health
- Coordinate with utility providers
- Reduce non-critical system load
- Escalate if instability persists
