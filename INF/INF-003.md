# INF-003 — ISP Backbone Congestion

## Rule Information

Rule ID: INF-003

Domain: Infrastructure Resilience

Category: Backbone Network Congestion

Status: Active

Version: 1.0

Repository: Sextant Rule Library

---

## Purpose

Evaluate system resilience under ISP backbone congestion conditions where upstream internet infrastructure becomes saturated, causing degraded connectivity across multiple services and regions.

---

## Scenario

Internet Service Provider (ISP) backbone routes experience severe congestion due to traffic spikes, routing inefficiencies, regional overload, or upstream infrastructure limitations. This leads to degraded connectivity between users, cloud systems, and data centres.

The scenario evaluates:

- Upstream bandwidth saturation
- Routing efficiency degradation
- Cross-region latency escalation
- Packet loss under congestion
- Service accessibility under load stress

---

## Primary Indicators

- ISP backbone utilization spikes
- Increased packet loss across regions
- Latency fluctuations
- Intermittent service reachability
- Routing instability (BGP fluctuations)
- Throughput degradation
- Connection timeouts

---

## Risk Levels

### GREEN

Network backbone operating within normal capacity.

No congestion detected.

No contingency actions required.

---

### YELLOW

Early backbone congestion detected.

Monitor:

- Rising latency
- Minor packet loss
- Traffic spikes

Action:

Monitor ISP performance and prepare traffic optimization strategies.

---

### ORANGE

Significant backbone congestion.

Indicators:

- Sustained packet loss
- Regional slowdowns
- High latency variance
- Partial service degradation

Potential Cascade:

Backbone Congestion

↓

Network Latency Increase

↓

Service Degradation

↓

Operational Impact

Action:

Optimize routing paths, reduce non-critical traffic, and engage ISP escalation channels.

---

### RED

Critical backbone saturation.

Indicators:

- Severe packet loss across regions
- Widespread service slowdown
- Routing instability
- Near-complete connectivity degradation

Potential Cascade:

Backbone Failure

↓

Regional Network Collapse

↓

Service Outage

↓

Cross-Domain Systemic Risk

Action:

Activate failover routing, shift traffic to alternative providers, and reduce system load aggressively.

---

## Cross-Domain Dependencies

Infrastructure (INF)

↓

Data Centre (DC)

↓

Cyber (CYB)

↓

Financial (FIN)

Backbone congestion impacts all higher-layer systems dependent on internet connectivity.

---

## Contingency Actions

- Reroute traffic through alternative ISP paths
- Reduce non-essential bandwidth usage
- Engage ISP escalation support
- Enable traffic prioritization (QoS)
- Monitor routing instability continuously
- Activate regional failover if required
