# DC-001 — Cooling Failure

## Rule Information

Rule ID: DC-001

Domain: Data Centre Resilience

Category: Thermal Management

Status: Active

Version: 1.0

Repository: Sextant Rule Library

---

## Purpose

Evaluate the resilience of a data centre during cooling system degradation or failure and assess the potential cascading effects across interconnected infrastructure, cyber, and financial domains.

---

## Scenario

A failure or degradation of the cooling system causes temperatures within the data centre to rise beyond normal operating thresholds, increasing the risk of equipment throttling, service degradation, and infrastructure failure.

The scenario evaluates:

- Cooling system performance
- Server temperature
- Rack temperature
- Power consumption
- System availability
- Environmental conditions

---

## Primary Indicators

- Server temperature
- Rack temperature
- Cooling unit status
- Chiller performance
- Airflow efficiency
- Humidity level
- Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE)

---

## Risk Levels

### GREEN

Cooling systems operating normally.

Temperature remains within design limits.

No contingency actions required.

---

### YELLOW

Early signs of cooling degradation.

Monitor:

- Temperature trends
- Cooling efficiency
- Fan performance
- PUE

Action:

Increase environmental monitoring and inspect cooling equipment.

---

### ORANGE

Significant cooling stress.

Indicators:

- Rising server temperatures
- Reduced cooling capacity
- Increased fan speeds
- Thermal hotspots detected

Potential Cascade:

Cooling Failure

↓

Temperature Rise

↓

CPU Throttling

↓

Application Performance Degradation

Action:

Activate contingency cooling procedures and reduce compute load where appropriate.

---

### RED

Critical cooling failure.

Indicators:

- Extreme temperatures
- Cooling system outage
- Thermal shutdown risk
- Critical infrastructure alarms

Potential Cascade:

Cooling Failure

↓

Thermal Shutdown

↓

Service Outage

↓

Infrastructure Failure

↓

Cross-Domain Systemic Risk

Action:

Activate emergency response procedures, protect critical infrastructure, and initiate disaster recovery where required.

---

## Cross-Domain Dependencies

Data Centre (DC)

↓

Infrastructure (INF)

↓

Cyber (CYB)

↓

Financial (FIN)

Cooling failures may affect digital services, operational continuity, and financial systems supported by the data centre.

---

## Contingency Actions

- Continuously monitor environmental conditions
- Activate backup cooling systems
- Reduce compute workloads
- Protect critical equipment
- Assess cascading operational impacts
- Escalate to incident management when thresholds are exceeded
